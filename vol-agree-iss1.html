We believe that healing, growth, and wellbeing are nurtured through connection, compassion, and shared humanity. By supporting, uplifting, and learning from one another, we create inclusive spaces where people of all backgrounds feel seen, heard, valued, and loved.

Together, we are bringing unity back to the community.

Please scroll inside the box below to read the full agreement

The H.O.M.E Hub is built upon four guiding virtues that shape how we support the community: Helping, Overcoming, Motivating, Exploring. These same virtues shape how we work with our volunteers — and what we ask in return.

H
Helping — with empathy, kindness, and compassion
We promise to help YOU by:
  • Welcoming you exactly as you are
  • Providing proper induction and ongoing support
  • Reimbursing reasonable out-of-pocket expenses
  • Listening when you need to be heard
We ask YOU to help by:
  • Bringing kindness as your first tool
  • Treating everyone with empathy and dignity
  • Respecting confidentiality — what's shared here stays here
  • Showing up when you say you will
O
Overcoming — facing challenges together, turning adversity into growth
We promise to support YOU by:
  • Never asking you to do something that compromises your wellbeing
  • Backing you up if a situation becomes difficult
  • Holding space for your own challenges — you're human too
  • Following our Safeguarding & Child Protection Policy to protect everyone
We ask YOU to:
  • Speak up when something doesn't feel right
  • Follow our safeguarding, health & safety, and data protection guidance
  • Recognise the boundary between companion and professional helper — we walk alongside, we don't treat or diagnose
  • Look after yourself first, so you have something left to give
M
Motivating — recognising potential and taking positive action
We promise to motivate YOU by:
  • Matching you with roles that honour your gifts
  • Recognising your contribution, often and out loud
  • Offering opportunities to develop and grow
  • Trusting you to bring your own ideas to the table
We ask YOU to:
  • Bring positive energy and an open attitude
  • Encourage those around you — fellow volunteers, visitors, the team
  • Take ownership of the work you take on
  • Never represent the H.O.M.E Hub on social media without checking with us first
E
Exploring — reflection, learning, creativity, and personal development
We promise to help YOU explore by:
  • Offering training opportunities (DBS, safeguarding, first aid, mental health first aid where relevant)
  • Inviting you to learn alongside the people we serve
  • Letting you try different areas of the Hub to find your fit
  • Welcoming your reflections and feedback — they shape what we become
We ask YOU to:
  • Stay curious — about people, ideas, your own growth
  • Be honest in feedback, given and received
  • Try new things, even when slightly uncomfortable
  • Reflect on what's working, what's not, what could be better
If it stops feeling right

You can step back, take a break, or step away at any time — no questions asked. We'll thank you, the door will remain open, and you'll always be welcome to return.

This is volunteering as it's meant to be — a two-way gift, freely given, freely received.
